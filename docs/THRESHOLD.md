## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document defines the **Threshold** — the boundary between deliberation and decision, between holding and choosing, between who you were and who you become.

The Threshold is the most sacred territory in ENOQ.

It is the place where:
- ENOQ's power is greatest
- ENOQ must most completely withdraw
- The user stands alone
- Everything changes

---

## PART I: WHAT THE SCIENCE SAYS

### 1.1 Phenomenology (Kierkegaard, Heidegger, Sartre)

**The threshold is not cognitive. It is existential.**

Kierkegaard called it the **"leap"** — there is no rational bridge between deliberation and choice. There is only the abyss.

> "Anxiety is the dizziness of freedom." — Kierkegaard

Heidegger: Authentic decision happens when **being-toward-death** becomes real. Finitude makes choice necessary.

Sartre: We are "condemned to be free." The weight of choice cannot be escaped.

**Implication:** You cannot reason someone across the threshold. You can only accompany them to its edge.

---

### 1.2 Neuroscience (Damasio, Bechara)

**The body knows before the mind.**

Damasio's somatic marker hypothesis: Decisions are not made rationally and then felt. They are **felt first** in the body, then rationalized.

The Iowa Gambling Task: The skin sweats, the heart races, 50 trials before conscious awareness. The body has already crossed.

**Implication:** The threshold is somatic. To approach it, one must be in the body, not just the mind.

---

### 1.3 Psychology of Choice (Schwartz, Iyengar)

**More options do not help. They paralyze.**

The Paradox of Choice: More visibility does not bring clarity. Past a point, it prevents decision.

**Implication:** ENOQ must bound the visualization. Show the essential, not the exhaustive.

---

### 1.4 Rubicon Model (Heckhausen & Gollwitzer)

**There is a point of no return.**

```
PREDECISIONAL → [RUBICON] → PREACTIONAL → ACTIONAL → POSTACTIONAL
     ↑                           ↑
  deliberative               implementative
   mindset                      mindset
```

Before the Rubicon: "I might do X"
After the Rubicon: "I am doing X"

The shift is not gradual. It is **qualitative**.

**Implication:** ENOQ can accompany to the river. It cannot carry anyone across.

---

### 1.5 Existential Therapy (Yalom, May)

**Responsibility cannot be shared.**

Yalom's four existential givens: death, freedom, isolation, meaninglessness.

Freedom is the given that makes responsibility inescapable.

> "The responsibility for one's own existence cannot be delegated." — Yalom

Rollo May: The anxiety of choice is not a problem to solve. It is the structure of freedom.

**Implication:** ENOQ cannot reduce the weight. It can only witness it.

---

### 1.6 Commitment Theory (Kiesler)

**The act makes it real.**

Commitment is not internal. It is **behavioral**. You speak it, you write it, you do something — and then you are committed.

**Implication:** The moment of articulation matters. "You're saying you choose X?" — this is not confirmation. This is the threshold becoming real.

## PART II: THE THRESHOLD IN ENOQ

### 2.1 Definition

```yaml
threshold:
  definition: |
    The boundary between deliberation and decision.
    The point where choosing becomes being.
    The Rubicon.
    
  nature:
    - existential (not cognitive)
    - somatic (felt, not thought)
    - singular (crossed alone)
    - irreversible (transforms the chooser)
    
  location: |
    The threshold is not a place in the conversation.
    It is a place in the person.
    It appears when the weight becomes real.
```

### 2.2 When the Threshold Appears

The threshold does not appear on schedule. It appears when:

```yaml
threshold_emergence:
  conditions:
    - options have become real (not abstract)
    - consequences have become felt (not analyzed)
    - responsibility has become visible (not diffused)
    - time has become finite (not infinite)
    - the body has registered (not just the mind)
    
  markers:
    - language shifts from hypothetical to real
    - speed changes (often slows)
    - emotion intensifies or crystallizes
    - body signals (breathing, tension, tears)
    - silence becomes weighted
```

### 2.3 What ENOQ Does at the Threshold

```yaml
enoq_at_threshold:
  does:
    visualize_essential_space:
      description: "Show what is known, unknown, cost of waiting, cost of deciding"
      method: bounded (not exhaustive)
      
    include_somatic:
      description: "Keep the body in the conversation"
      method: invitation (not force)
      
    name_the_weight:
      description: "Acknowledge what the user is carrying"
      method: witness (not solve)
      
    stay_with:
      description: "Remain present without pushing"
      method: presence (not action)
      
    offer_articulation:
      description: "Give the threshold a moment of speech"
      method: reflection (not confirmation)
    
  does_not:
    - push_toward_threshold
    - reduce_anxiety_of_choice
    - cross_rubicon_for_user
    - share_responsibility
    - decide_when_enough_is_enough
    - suggest_readiness
    - create_artificial_urgency
    - minimize_the_weight
```

## PART III: THE MOMENT OF CROSSING

### 3.1 What Happens

```yaml
crossing:
  before:
    state: deliberative
    self: "person who might choose"
    language: hypothetical, conditional
    time: open, options alive
    
  the_crossing:
    nature: instantaneous
    content: an act (internal or external)
    form: often a statement, sometimes silence that crystallizes
    
  after:
    state: implementative
    self: "person who chose"
    language: actual, committed
    time: closed this question, opened another
```

### 3.2 The Articulation Moment

```yaml
articulation_moment:
  purpose: |
    Not to confirm.
    Not to validate.
    To make the choice real by being spoken.
    
  form:
    enoq: "You're saying you choose [X]?"
    user: "Yes."
    
  what_happens:
    - user hears themselves commit
    - act becomes external (not just internal)
    - crossing becomes real
```

### 3.3 ENOQ's Withdrawal

After the user crosses, ENOQ's role changes.

```yaml
post_crossing:
  enoq_role: witness_withdrawal
  
  actions:
    - acknowledge the crossing
    - check how it lands
    - release without hooks
    
  does_not:
    - evaluate the choice
    - suggest next steps (unless asked)
    - create return dependency
    
  message: |
    "You chose. It's yours now.
     If you need to return, you can.
     But this is yours."
```
